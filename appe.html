Recipe Name: Rice Appe

Estimated Calorie Intake: Approximately 100 calories per serving

Health Benefits: Rice Appe is a healthy and versatile dish that is low in calories and high in nutritional value. It provides a good source of carbohydrates, protein, and essential nutrients. The fermentation process involved in making appe enhances its digestibility and promotes gut health. Additionally, the use of minimal oil makes it a lighter and nutritious snack.

Detailed Instructions:

  1. Soak rice and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind soaked urad dal to a smooth batter. Similarly, grind soaked rice to a coarse batter.
  3. Mix both batters, add salt, and allow it to ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  4. Add finely chopped vegetables like carrots, capsicum, and onions to the batter.
  5. Heat an appe pan, add a few drops of oil in each mold.
  6. Pour spoonfuls of batter into each mold and cook on medium heat until the bottom turns golden brown.
  7. Flip the appe using a wooden skewer and cook the other side until golden brown and cooked through.
  8. Remove from the pan and serve hot with chutney or sauce of your choice.
